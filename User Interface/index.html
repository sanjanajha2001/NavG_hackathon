Websites often include forms as a part of their customer data collection strategy. A well-made
survey form can help you acquire relevant information about your target audiences like their
demographic age, job, location, taste and preference, and pain points. This HTML project is a
great way to test your skills and knowledge of designing forms and structuring a webpage.

Building a survey form is no rocket science. You need to acquaint yourself with the basic
tags/input fields in HTML required to design forms. Then you can use the tags to create a text
field, checkbox, radio button, date, and other vital elements contained in a form. Again, you can
always use CSS to impart a better look and feel to your form and webpage.
